Complete System Restore

In most cases this means resetting your computer to a "like new state" by backing up your data, reinstalling the operating system and restoring your data. This erases everything good and bad and gives you a fresh copy of windows or whatever operating system you are running.

This is a great way to get your system running like new again, but you may lose some of your old programs (for most people this isn't an issue you can replace your old dusty programs with shiny new ones).

Complete System Tune-up

Same basic idea as a complete system restore or a virus removal. Except we don't delete everything like in a system restore and you don't need to have a virus you just need your computer to work like it used to. (This will not make an old outdated computer into an amazing super computer. For that I may recomend some software downgrades or hardware upgrades if the cost of the upgrades are less then a replacement computer)

90% of the time you want to go with the Complete System Restore instead of the tune-up or even virus removal. Since restore deletes everything making sure there is no hidden malware and the tune-up is an atempt to remove anything slowing down your system but can leave behind undetected malware.
